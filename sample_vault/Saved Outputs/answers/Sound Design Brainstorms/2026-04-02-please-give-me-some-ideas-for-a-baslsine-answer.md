## Sound Design Brainstorm

Quick Answer:  
For a progressive house track with an FM synth bassline, focus on syncopated, filtered patterns that avoid occupying the kick's space. Use layering, velocity, and side-chaining to carve space and maintain groove.

---

### **Production Recipes**  

#### **Recipe 1: Syncopated Filtered Bassline**  
**Groove / MIDI**  
- **Bar Length**: 4/4  
- **Timing / Step Grid**: Quarter notes on beats 2 & 4, eighth notes on beat 1.  
- **Pitch**: C3 (root) on beat 1, B2 (b5) on beat 2, C3 on beat 4.  
- **Note Length**: Quarter notes, with velocity swells on beat 1 to emphasize filter cut-off.  

**Sound Design**  
- **Oscillators**: FM synth mode with harmonics softened (reduce upper partials).  
- **Filter**: 4-pole low-pass with cutoff velocity-sensitive (opens on higher notes).  
- **Envelope**: Slow attack (0.5s) and release (2s) to allow filtering during notes.  

**How to Build It**  
1. Map velocity to filter cutoff in your synth.  
2. Use an LFO to modulate filter cutoff slowly (0-20% range).  
3. Add a sub-octave layer (C1) with low-pass filter for weight.  

**Where to Use It**  
- Drop, build sections, or main body of the track.  

---

#### **Recipe 2: Ghost Notes & Octave Layers**  
**Groove / MIDI**  
- **Bar Length**: 4/4  
- **Timing / Step Grid**: Half-notes on beats 1 & 3, dotted quarter notes on beat 2.  
- **Pitch**: G3 (5th) on beat 1, F3 (4th) on beat 2, G3 on beat 3.  
- **Note Length**: Half-notes with swing quantization (60% swing).  

**Sound Design**  
- **Oscillators**: FM mode with detuning (-2/+2 semitones).  
- **Filter**: High-pass (500Hz) with resonance, synced to LFO.  
- **Saturation**: Apply analog-style clipping to add grit.  

**How to Build It**  
1. Duplicate the MIDI clip and transpose one layer an octave higher.  
2. Automate filter cutoff to open during the kick’s absence.  
3. Use a sampler with a kick sample removed for rhythmic clarity.  

**Where to Use It**  
- Breakdowns or transitions to add texture without occupying space.  

---

#### **Recipe 3: Arpeggiated Bassline**  
**Groove / MIDI**  
- **Bar Length**: 4/4  
- **Timing / Step Grid**: Sixteenth notes across the bar (C3-E3-G3-B2).  
- **Pitch**: Arpeggio pattern starting on C3, moving up a minor third each bar.  
- **Note Length**: Sixteenth notes with velocity variation (alternate 80-90).  

**Sound Design**  
- **Oscillators**: FM mode with waveform set to “sawtooth.”  
- **Envelope**: Fast attack (0.1s) and short decay (0.3s) for punch.  
- **Modulation**: Add a chorus effect for movement.  

**How to Build It**  
1. Map the arpeggiator to follow the MIDI clip.  
2. Sync the arpeggiator speed to the track tempo (e.g., 1 bar per cycle).  
3. Layer a simple kick-accenting sub-bass (C1) for weight.  

**Where to Use It**  
- Builds or intros for melodic emphasis.  

---

### **Optional Variations**  
1. **Sub-Bass Layer**: Add a second FM synth layer an octave lower (C2) with a high-pass filter (800Hz) for sub-bass weight.  
2. **Staccato Stabs**: Replace longer notes with staccato hits (100ms length) for percussive impact during builds.  
3. **Percussive Elements**: Use the synth’s noise generator to create hi-hat-like accents on off-beats.  

These patterns prioritize groove, space, and genre-native layering to complement your kick and FM synth patch.
