## Sound Design Brainstorm

Quick Answer  
Start with a simple 1-bar rhythmic bass pattern locked tightly with the kick’s downbeat, then add a second layer with offbeat accents and slight melodic movement to create groove and energy, using your FM synth patch with filter modulated by velocity or envelope.

Production Recipes

Name: Classic Progressive House Driving Groove  
Groove / MIDI: 1 bar loop on 4/4, notes synced to quarter notes: bass note on beat 1 (kick hit), then short staccato bass notes on the “and” of beats 2 and 3, like: 1, 2&, 3&, 4 (rest). Note length ~1/16 to keep space. Velocity slightly stronger on beat 1, softer on offbeats.  
Pitch: Root note on beat 1, adding 5th (perfect fifth) on 2& and octave drop on 3& for variation. E.g., C2 (beat 1), G1 (2&), C1 (3&).  
Sound Design: Use your FM patch with a lowpass filter modulated by velocity so accented notes open the filter slightly. Keep the envelope with a fast attack, medium decay, no sustain, short release for punch. Add mild saturation or soft distortion to fatten the low end without cluttering the mix.  
How to Build It:  
1. In DAW piano roll, draw a 1-bar midi clip at 4/4.  
2. Insert C2 on beat 1, G1 on 2&, C1 on 3&, leave rest elsewhere.  
3. Set note lengths short (~1/16 note) for tightness.  
4. Program varying velocities: highest on beat 1, lower on offbeat notes.  
5. Assign filter cutoff to velocity control in your FM synth.  
6. Add light saturation plugin on bass channel.  
Where to Use It: Ideal for main groove in early drop and main sections to lock with kick and drive the rhythm.

Name: Two-Layer Accented Groove with Syncopation  
Groove / MIDI: Layer 1 plays quarter notes on beats 1 and 3 (kick hits), Layer 2 has rhythmic 16th-note offbeat accents starting on the ‘e’ of beat 2 and ‘a’ of beat 4 (notes at 16th note subdivisions: 2e, 4a). Keep Layer 2 notes shorter and simpler rhythmically to avoid clutter.  
Pitch: Layer 1 root note (e.g., C2) on beats 1 and 3; Layer 2 plays fifths or minor third (e.g., G2 or Eb2) accented on the offbeat subdivisions.  
Sound Design: Layer 1 uses your FM bass patch with a tight, warm filter envelope; Layer 2 uses a simpler sine or triangle wave with subtle low-pass filtering and less distortion for contrast. Apply sidechain compression keyed to the kick only on Layer 1 for punch and clarity.  
How to Build It:  
1. Create two MIDI clips for bass layers.  
2. Layer 1: notes on beats 1 and 3, C2, with full length (half note).  
3. Layer 2: notes on 16th notes 2e and 4a, play G2 or Eb2 with length ~1/16 note.  
4. Set Layer 2 velocity lower than Layer 1 for subtlety.  
5. Map filter cutoff or FM index modulation to velocity or keytracking on both synth patches.  
6. Insert sidechain compressor on Layer 1 bass channel triggered by kick drum.  
Where to Use It: Use for building tension in breakdown returns or pre-drop sections where rhythm needs more intricacy but not overload.

Optional Variations

- Add velocity-controlled filter modulation on accented notes for dynamic groove shifts during pumps or transitions.  
- Try sliding the offbeat notes slightly delayed (2-10ms) in timing for a looser human feel.  
- For an ambient breakdown, soften bass filter cutoff and reduce velocity on all notes to create a gentle rumble under pads.

These patterns fit a typical 122-128 BPM progressive house groove, complement your FM synth patch by layering and velocity modulation, and keep the low end clean for the kick to sit well in the mix [Import 1/2][Local 1].
